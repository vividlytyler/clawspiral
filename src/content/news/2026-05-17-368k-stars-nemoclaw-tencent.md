May 2026 marks a turning point for OpenClaw. Three developments landed in quick succession that collectively signal the project has outgrown its scrappy open-source roots and entered the enterprise spotlight.

## The Numbers

The [openclaw/openclaw](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw) repository crossed **368,000 GitHub stars** and **12 million downloads** — a milestone that would be unremarkable for a large company but is exceptional for a community-driven project that launched as "Clawdbot" in late 2025.

## NVIDIA NemoClaw Alpha

NVIDIA shipped the alpha release of **NemoClaw**, a hardened OpenClaw fork integrated with NeMo guardrails and OpenShell sandboxes. The fork is aimed at risk-averse enterprise buyers who need something defensible in architecture reviews — specifically, organizations that need to show a credible security story to procurement and compliance teams.

## Tencent Commits Full-Time Maintainers

Tencent formally committed full-time maintainers to the project, bringing significant resources to the OpenClaw ecosystem. This follows earlier reports of Tencent's involvement and signals a level of institutional commitment that most open-source AI agent projects never achieve.

## The Anthropic Friction

In the background, the friction with Anthropic continues to simmer. Reports emerged that Claude Code detects HERMES.md files (the OpenClaw agent configuration manifest) and either refuses requests or routes them to higher-cost billing tiers — with users reporting cost increases of up to 50x. The Hacker News thread accumulated 1,336 upvotes and 718 comments. Anthropic's head of Claude Code, Boris Cherny, was quoted: *"Anthropic's subscriptions weren't built for the usage patterns of these third-party tools."*

For enterprise IT teams evaluating self-hosted AI agent platforms, the question is no longer "is OpenClaw real?" — it's "what is our deployment plan?"
