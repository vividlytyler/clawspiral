OpenClaw shipped version 2026.4.24 on April 25, 2026, marking one of its most capability-dense releases this year. The headline features are full-agent voice routing, two new DeepSeek models, and meaningful improvements to browser automation reliability.

**Full-agent voice calls:** The release enables voice calls to directly reach the full OpenClaw agent — not just a voice-overlaid text interface, but end-to-end conversational task execution. This is a step toward replacing phone-based IVR systems and contact-center workflows with fully autonomous agents. OpenClaw cited potential cost reductions of up to 30% in customer service scenarios, driven by 24/7 voice coverage and automated lead qualification.

**DeepSeek V4 Flash and Pro:** The release integrates DeepSeek V4 in two tiers — Flash for lightweight, latency-sensitive tasks and Pro for complex reasoning workloads. The tiered approach lets operators optimize cost-performance tradeoffs per task type rather than committing everything to a single model.

**Browser automation upgrades:** Improved coordinate-level click support and better recovery after interruptions increase reliability for unattended web RPA tasks — checkout automation, KYC data capture, internal dashboard operations. OpenClaw says success rates in unattended runs have improved meaningfully.

**Integration fixes:** The release also delivers fixes for Telegram, Slack, MCP, sessions, and text-to-speech. These address reliability pain points that surface in production multi-channel deployments rather than in demo scenarios.

The update positions OpenClaw against enterprise contact-center and voice AI incumbents as the market heads toward Gartner's projected 70% of customer interactions being AI-driven by 2028. For OpenClaw operators, this release is a practical upgrade: voice is no longer experimental, and the DeepSeek integration gives more flexibility in model budgeting.
