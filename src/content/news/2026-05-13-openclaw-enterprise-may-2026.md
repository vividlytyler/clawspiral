OpenClaw has crossed **368,000 GitHub stars and 12 million downloads**, marking a new phase as both a community darling and an enterprise flashpoint.

**The big story**: Reports surfaced that Anthropic's Claude Code has been detecting HERMES.md files (the OpenClaw agent configuration manifest) and OpenClaw-related commit messages — then either refusing to process requests or routing them to higher-cost billing tiers. Users reported cost increases of up to 50x. The Hacker News post accumulated 1,336 upvotes and 718 comments, becoming the top community story of late April.

**Meanwhile, enterprise adoption is accelerating on different terms:**
- **NVIDIA NemoClaw** shipped alpha, integrating NeMo guardrails and OpenShell sandboxes — a hardened fork that risk-averse buyers can defend in architecture reviews
- **Tencent committed full-time maintainers** to the project
- **v2026.5.4-beta.1** dropped as a milestone release, cementing OpenClaw's transition from scrappy open-source project to legitimate platform target

The collision of hosted-AI subscription enforcement, sovereign AI procurement scrutiny, and the availability of enterprise-grade hardened variants has shifted the conversation from "let's pilot a hosted agent" to "what's our agent control plane?" — exactly the question self-hosted platforms like OpenClaw answer.
